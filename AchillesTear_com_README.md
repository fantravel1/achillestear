## 4) The Achilles Prevention Framework (6 Pillars)

This is the system that anchors the entire site.

### Pillar 1 — Tendon Strength (Capacity)
The Achilles responds best to structured loading:
- isometrics
- heavy slow resistance (HSR)
- eccentrics (especially for tendinopathy)
- soleus + gastrocnemius programming
- foot intrinsic strengthening

**Key concept:** Tendons adapt slower than muscles.

---

### Pillar 2 — Form & Mechanics (The Differentiator)
Achilles tears often occur during:
- sprint takeoff
- hard stops
- jump landings
- cutting and direction change
- unexpected slips/lunges

AchillesTear.com becomes famous by teaching:
- sprint mechanics (no overstride)
- landing stiffness
- deceleration control
- foot collapse under fatigue
- hip control → foot control connection

---

### Pillar 3 — Load Management
Most tears involve load spikes:
- season start
- return after vacation
- shoe changes
- surface changes
- sudden sprint volume

AchillesTear.com teaches:
- weekly progression rules
- return-to-sport ramps
- deload patterns

---

### Pillar 4 — Mobility & Stretching (Correctly Framed)
Stretching is a tool — not the solution.

AchillesTear.com teaches:
- mobility where it matters (ankle dorsiflexion)
- when stretching helps vs irritates
- why strength + mobility must be paired

---

### Pillar 5 — Recovery Systems
Tendon resilience is built 24/7:
- sleep quality
- rest days
- warm-up quality
- cooldown & stiffness reduction
- fatigue monitoring

---

### Pillar 6 — Nutrition + Hydration
Tendon health is influenced by:
- under-fueling / low energy availability
- protein adequacy
- micronutrient sufficiency
- dehydration + cramping risk
- alcohol and sleep disruption

AchillesTear.com provides athlete-friendly fueling basics.
