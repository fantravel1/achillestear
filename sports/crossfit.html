Movement-Specific Protection

Protecting Your Achilles During CrossFit Movements

High-risk movements and how to manage them.

Box Jumps & Jump Training

  • Always focus on landing control (quiet landing = safe landing).
  • Start with lower box heights, build up gradually over weeks.
  • Full recovery between sets ‚Äî never do failing jump reps.
  • Eccentric heel drops + box jump training = optimal Achilles preparation.

Double-Unders & Jump Rope

  • High-rep jump rope loads the Achilles cumulatively.
  • In high-rep WODs, limit doubles to 50‚Äì100 reps per workout initially.
  • Build jump rope volume over 4‚Äì6 weeks, never spike.
  • Maintain daily eccentric heel drops + jump rope = protected tendons.

Heavy Olympic Lifts (Snatch, Clean)

  • Explosive pulling demands large eccentric forces through the Achilles.
  • Light eccentric loading before heavy lifting = protective priming.
  • Never increase snatch/clean volume more than 10% per week.
  • Maintain strength work 3x/week alongside Olympic lifting.

Metabolic Conditioning (MetCons)

  • Back-to-back high-intensity efforts accumulate Achilles fatigue.
  • Warm-up with 5 minutes of calf activation before MetCons.
  • During high-volume rep schemes, focus on movement quality over speed.
  • If feeling Achilles stiffness, reduce intensity ‚Äî don't push through.
