When Achilles Tears Happen in Tennis

Most occur during explosive serve/pushoff and rapid court transitions.

The Explosive Serve Pushoff

Loading into your serve, your back leg generates massive calf power. The Achilles must handle extreme eccentric load as you push off. This is the #1 tear moment in tennis.

The Fix: Heavy slow resistance calf loading + serving-specific plyometrics.

The Side-to-Side Sprint

Explosive sideways movement to reach a wide shot. Your planted foot must resist lateral force while generating explosive pushoff. High-speed direction change = high Achilles stress.

The Fix: Lateral bounds + lateral lunges with eccentric focus.

The Quick Stop & Start

Tennis demands rapid acceleration and deceleration. Your Achilles must control the deceleration phase when you stop hard, then instantly re-accelerate. Repetitive high-load cycles.

The Fix: Eccentric loading + plyometric bounds with focus on control.

The Long Rally Fatigue

Extended rallies = repeated high-load cycles on the Achilles. As fatigue accumulates, proprioception drops, mechanics break down, Achilles injury risk spikes.

The Fix: Specific conditioning work + mechanics training under fatigue.
Pre-Match Routine

The Tennis Achilles Activation (12 Minutes)

Do this on-court, before practice or match. Primes your Achilles for explosive movement.

1

Ankle Mobility Series (2 min)

Ankle circles, alphabet footwriting, then single-leg balance. 30 seconds per exercise per leg. Courts are uneven ‚Äî your ankle needs mobile awareness.

2

Calf Pumps & Holds (2 min)

Rise on toes 30 times rapid, then hold at the top for 45 seconds. 3 sets. Activates and primes the Achilles for explosive loading.

3

Single-Leg Balance (2 min)

30 seconds per leg, add eye closure halfway through. This builds ankle proprioception ‚Äî critical for tennis stability.

4

Lateral Shuffles (2 min)

Side-to-side shuffle steps across the baseline, 20 meters each direction. Build speed gradually. Prepares your Achilles for lateral court movement.

5

Serve Simulation (2 min)

Stand in your serving stance, do slow-motion serve movements with no racket. Build into 50% speed, then 75% speed. Activates the specific muscles used in your serve.

6

Light Court Sprints (2 min)

Diagonal sprints across the court at 70% intensity. 4 sprints. Your Achilles is now warm, mobile, and ready for match intensity.

Off-Court Training

Tennis Achilles Strength Block (12 Weeks)

3‚Äì4 sessions per week, 30 minutes each. Off-season to pre-season progression.

Weeks 1‚Äì4: Foundational Loading

Eccentric Heel Drops ‚Äî 3 sets of 15 reps, daily. The baseline for all Achilles training.
Isometric Calf Holds ‚Äî 3 sets of 45 seconds, 3x/week. Hold at the top, engage the Achilles.
Single-Leg Calf Raises ‚Äî 3 sets of 12 reps per leg, 3x/week. Perfect form, full range of motion.
Soleus-Specific Work ‚Äî 3 sets of 20 reps, 3x/week. Seated calf raises with knee bent 90¬∞. The often-overlooked protector.

Weeks 5‚Äì8: Court-Specific Loading

Weeks 9‚Äì12: Serve-Specific Power

Lateral Bounds ‚Äî 4 sets of 10 reps per side, 2x/week. Explosive lateral power. Land soft, control descent.
