Protect Your Achilles At the Net.

Serve & Volley-Specific Risk

Position Demands: When Achilles Tears Happen for Serve & Volley Players

Power serve mechanics, immediate net approach, reflex volleys, and emergency reach shots define the serve-and-volley Achilles risk profile.

🚀

The Power Serve

A jump serve generates up to 4 times bodyweight through the Achilles at ball contact. The explosive plantarflexion and subsequent landing both load the tendon at extreme force levels — the defining high-risk moment for this style of play.

The Fix: Eccentric heel drops daily and isometric holds 3×/week to build the tendon stiffness required for maximum-effort serves.
🏃

Serve-to-Sprint Transition

Immediately sprinting forward toward the net after completing the serve. The Achilles must switch from the explosive plantarflexion of the serve to the acceleration mechanics of a forward sprint with no recovery time between.

The Fix: Progressive speed-to-net drills 2×/week to train the tendon to manage the serve-to-sprint force transition.
🎾

The Net Volley Split-Step

A short-range split-step at the net position with minimal reaction time between the opponent's shot and the required movement. The compressed reaction window reduces the Achilles's ability to load progressively.

The Fix: Reactive hop training 2×/week to build reflex-speed tendon activation at net-position distances.
↔️

The Emergency Reach

Reaching wide for a passing shot when the opponent reads the net approach. The lunge to a wide ball from a forward net position places the Achilles at an extreme plantarflexion angle under high force.

The Fix: Lateral lunge stability training to reinforce the Achilles through wide-reach positions.
Pre-Match Routine

Serve & Volley Achilles Activation (12 Minutes)

Complete on-court before every match or practice session. Primes the Achilles for serve forces and net-approach sprints.

1

Serve Simulation (3 min)

Controlled serve-motion swings at 50% then 70% effort without sprinting forward. 20 reps. Progressively loads the Achilles through the serve plantarflexion arc before full-effort serving.

2

Net Approach Sprint Activation (3 min)

Start at the baseline, serve motion, then sprint to the service line. 8 reps at 70% serve effort. Trains the serve-to-sprint transition that defines the style and the injury risk.

3

Reflex Hop Drills (2 min)

Standing at net position, perform small reactive split-steps as a partner signals direction. 20 reps. Prepares the Achilles for the compressed-reaction-window demands of net play.

4

Calf Pumps and Hold (2 min)

30 rapid toe raises followed by a 30-second hold at the top. 2 sets. Fully activates the calf-Achilles complex before serve-intensity loading.

5

Lateral Lunge Preparation (2 min)

Controlled lateral lunges to a wide-reach position. 12 reps each side at 60% effort. Prepares the Achilles for the emergency-reach mechanics of net play.

Off-Court Training

Serve & Volley Achilles Strength Block (12 Weeks)

3–4 sessions per week, 30 minutes each. Focused on serve plantarflexion power and net-approach sprint capacity.

Weeks 1–4: Foundational Loading

Eccentric Heel Drops — 3 sets of 15 reps, daily. The baseline for all Achilles training.
Isometric Calf Hold — 3 sets of 45 seconds, 3×/week. Rise on toes, hold. Builds tendon stiffness.
Single-Leg Calf Raises — 3 sets of 12 reps per leg, 3×/week. Full range of motion, full control.
Soleus-Specific Work — 3 sets of 20 reps, 3×/week. Seated calf raises with knee bent 90°. The often-overlooked protector.

Weeks 5–8: Court-Specific Loading

Weighted Eccentric Heel Drops — Add 15 lbs. 3 sets of 12 reps, 3×/week. Increased load accelerates tendon adaptation.
Heavy Slow Resistance — 3 sets of 12 reps at 75% effort, 3×/week. Use stairs plus a weighted vest.
Lateral Lunges with Eccentric Focus — 3 sets of 10 reps per side, 2×/week. Slow the return phase. Mimics lateral court movement.
Single-Leg Balance with Arm Reach — 3 sets of 30 seconds per leg while reaching across the body. Adds proprioceptive challenge.

Weeks 9–12: Position-Specific Power

Lateral Bounds — 4 sets of 10 reps per side, 2×/week. Explosive lateral power, land soft, control descent.
Split-Step Plyometrics — 3 sets of 10 reps, 2×/week. Jump and land simultaneously on both feet, immediately react laterally.
Single-Leg Hops for Distance — 3 sets of 6 reps per leg, 2×/week. Builds explosive Achilles power.
Eccentric Heel Drops (Maintenance) — Continue 3 sets of 15, 3×/week. Never stop.
