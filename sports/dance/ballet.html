Dance — Ballet

Protect Your Achilles On the Barre and Beyond.

Ballet demands more from the Achilles than almost any other performing art. Pointe work compresses the insertional Achilles, repetitive relevés load the mid-portion across thousands of reps per week, and grand jeté landings create single-leg peak force events. The prevention approach must address all three.

Ballet-Specific Risk

When Achilles Problems Develop in Ballet

Ballet creates a unique combination of insertional compression and mid-portion tensile loading. Both must be addressed in prevention.

Full Pointe Loading

Maximum plantarflexion in pointe shoes places the Achilles at its shortest position while simultaneously compressing the Haglund’s area against the posterior heel. Unlike mid-portion tendinopathy, insertional Achilles problems in ballet dancers are driven by compression — not tension.

The Fix: Avoid full heel drop eccentrics for insertional problems. Use heel-elevated eccentrics (on a flat surface or slight heel raise) instead. Consult a physiotherapist for insertional-specific rehabilitation.

Relevé Repetition Volume

A professional ballet class involves hundreds of relevés across barre work, centre exercises, and combinations. Each relevé loads the mid-portion Achilles under the full body weight through plantarflexion. Volume accumulation across a week of training is enormous.

The Fix: Volume monitoring per session. Heavy slow resistance calf training 3×/week to build capacity ahead of class demands.

Grand Jeté Landing

Landing from a grand jeté on a single leg creates peak plantarflexion force events. The combination of height, forward momentum, and single-leg landing creates a loading profile similar to the sports-related scenarios that drive most athletic Achilles ruptures.

The Fix: Single-leg landing training + quiet foot drills. Practice soft, controlled landings from progressively increasing heights.

Deep Plié Demand

Full turnout deep pliés place the Achilles in a maximally lengthened position under body weight. The ankle dorsiflexion demanded in a full plié challenges Achilles flexibility at its limit — without adequate mobility, compensatory movement patterns load the tendon asymmetrically.

The Fix: Ankle mobility protocol + eccentric heel drops. Address dorsiflexion range before class demands expose the deficit.
Pre-Training Protocol

Ballet Achilles Activation (10 Minutes)

Complete before every class and performance. Progresses from parallel position to full turnout.

1

Ankle Circles in Parallel (2 min)

10 slow circles each direction per foot in parallel position. Reduces initial tendon stiffness before turnout demands.

2

Relevé Progression — Parallel to Turnout (3 min)

8 slow parallel relevés, then 8 in first position, then 8 in second. Progressive range activation before barre work.

3

Plié Range Warm-Up (2 min)

5 slow demi-pliés then 3 controlled grand pliés per side. Prepares the Achilles for the full range demands of barre.

4

Single-Leg Balance in Turnout (2 min)

30 s per leg in pass√© position. Activates the stabilisation required for all single-leg ballet movements.

5

Progressive Jump Warm-Up (1 min)

5 sautés then 5 assemblés at reduced effort. Graduated loading before any allegro combinations.

Training Protocol

Ballet Achilles Strength Block (12 Weeks)

3 sessions per week, 20–25 minutes. Progressive overload every 2 weeks.

Weeks 1–4: Tendon Capacity Base

Eccentric Heel Drops — 3×15 daily, straight-leg on a step. Lower over 4 seconds on one foot.
Isometric Calf Hold — 3×45 s, 3×/week. Rise on toes, hold at top. Builds tendon stiffness.
Seated Calf Raises — 3×20, 3×/week. Knee bent 90°. Targets the soleus specifically.
Single-Leg Balance — 3×30 s per leg. Builds proprioception and ankle stability.

Weeks 5–8: Power Development

Weighted Eccentric Heel Drops — Add 10 lb in a backpack. 3×12 daily. Accelerated tendon adaptation.
Heavy Slow Resistance — 3×12 at 70–80% effort, 3×/week. Maximum collagen-synthesis stimulus.
Pogo Hops — 3×30 s, 2×/week. Rapid ankle-only hops. Builds reactive stiffness.
Lateral Bounds — 3×8 per side, 2×/week. Explosive direction-change loading.

Weeks 9–12: Performance-Specific

Single-Leg Hops for Distance — 3×5 per leg, 2×/week. Power and control combined.
Plyometric Box Step-Offs — 3×6, 2×/week. Step down from 30 cm. Landing control focus.
Sport-Specific Movement Drills — 10 min, 2×/week. Replicate the exact movement patterns of your discipline.
Eccentric Heel Drops (Maintenance) — 3×15, 3×/week. Never stop — this is your performance maintenance.
