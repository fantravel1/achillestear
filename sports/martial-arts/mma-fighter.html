Protect Your Achilles Across Every Discipline.

MMA Fighter Risk

When Achilles Problems Develop in MMA Training

MMA’s multi-discipline approach creates four compounding Achilles risk scenarios unique to the sport.

The Multi-Discipline Training Day

Morning striking session + afternoon grappling + evening conditioning creates a compound Achilles loading day that no single-sport athlete experiences. Each session adds incremental load that wouldn’t be problematic alone — but combined, exceeds the tendon’s daily recovery capacity.

The Fix: Session load management: track training hours per discipline per week. Mandatory deload weeks every 4th week of camp.
üß±

Cage Wrestling Mechanics

Wrestling against the cage places the feet in unusual angles relative to the body — toes turned in, heels elevated, ankle in extreme inversion — under the full force of both athletes. The cage creates Achilles loading positions that free-wrestling and BJJ never require.

The Fix: Specific ankle stability training: single-leg balance with varied foot positions. Include cage-specific footwork drills in warm-up.

Weight Cut Effects on Tendon Tissue

Dehydration from making weight reduces tendon tissue water content — and collagen fibres need hydration for optimal mechanical properties. Post-cut rehydration restores most function but the window between weigh-in and fight when the athlete is dehydrated but already competing is high-risk.

The Fix: Gradual weight management rather than severe water cutting. Optimal hydration in the weeks leading to camp. Consider how dehydration affects training safety.
üìÖ

Fight Camp Volume Spike

Fight camp typically increases training volume 40–60% above base levels in the final 8–10 weeks. This concentrated volume increase, combined with multi-discipline training, creates ideal conditions for tendinopathy development — especially if eccentric maintenance is not prioritised.

The Fix: 10% per week maximum volume increase rule — even in camp. Eccentric heel drops 3×/week are non-negotiable throughout camp.
Pre-Training Protocol

MMA Fighter Achilles Activation (12 Minutes)

Complete before every training session — especially before multi-discipline days.

1

Ankle Mobility (2 min)

Circles, alphabet, inversion and eversion. Cover the full range used across striking stance, wrestling stance, and guard position.

2

Calf Pumps + Pogo Hops (3 min)

3×30 calf pumps, rest 20 s, then 3×20 s pogo hops. Activates both sustained bouncing (striking) and reactive elastic capacity (grappling transitions).

3

Striking Footwork Warm-Up (2 min)

Shadow boxing at 40% intensity — footwork only. Activates the bounce and pivot patterns before bag or pad work.

4

Takedown Shot Simulation (2 min)

3 slow controlled shots per side at 30% effort. Activates deep squat Achilles loading before grappling demands.

5

Cage Footwork Drill (3 min)

Against a wall, practice cage-wrestling stance with varied foot positions. Activates the unusual ankle angles demanded by cage work.

Training Protocol

MMA Fighter Achilles Strength Block (12 Weeks)

3 sessions per week, 20–25 minutes. Progressive overload every 2 weeks.

Weeks 1–4: Tendon Capacity Base

Eccentric Heel Drops — 3×15 daily, straight-leg on a step. Lower over 4 seconds on one foot.
Isometric Calf Hold — 3×45 s, 3×/week. Rise on toes, hold at top. Builds tendon stiffness.
Seated Calf Raises — 3×20, 3×/week. Knee bent 90°. Targets the soleus specifically.
Single-Leg Balance — 3×30 s per leg. Builds proprioception and ankle stability.

Weeks 5–8: Power Development

Weighted Eccentric Heel Drops — Add 10 lb in a backpack. 3×12 daily. Accelerated tendon adaptation.
Heavy Slow Resistance — 3×12 at 70–80% effort, 3×/week. Maximum collagen-synthesis stimulus.
Pogo Hops — 3×30 s, 2×/week. Rapid ankle-only hops. Builds reactive stiffness.
Lateral Bounds — 3×8 per side, 2×/week. Explosive direction-change loading.

Weeks 9–12: Performance-Specific

Single-Leg Hops for Distance — 3×5 per leg, 2×/week. Power and control combined.
Plyometric Box Step-Offs — 3×6, 2×/week. Step down from 30 cm. Landing control focus.
Sport-Specific Movement Drills — 10 min, 2×/week. Replicate the exact movement patterns of your discipline.
Eccentric Heel Drops (Maintenance) — 3×15, 3×/week. Never stop — this is your performance maintenance.
