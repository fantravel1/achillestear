Protect Your Achilles From Grip to Throw.

Judo’s explosive throw entries demand single-leg pivot forces that rival those in field athletics. The tsugi-ashi sliding approach, uchi-mata entry sweep, and randori continuous movement all create distinct Achilles loading patterns that judo-specific preparation must address.

Judoka-Specific Risk

When Achilles Problems Develop in Judo

Four judo-specific loading scenarios define the judoka’s Achilles risk in training and competition.

The Throw Entry

Explosive uchi-mata or seoi-nage entry requires a single-leg pivot that generates peak Achilles force comparable to field athletics takeoffs. The entry must be fast enough to unbalance uke before a defensive reaction — meaning there is no time to build force gradually.

The Fix: Eccentric drops + single-leg strength work. Build unilateral Achilles capacity before throw volume increases.

The Tsugi-Ashi Approach

The sliding entry footwork used in major throws involves rapid shuffle steps that load the Achilles laterally through the sliding motion. Repeated across thousands of randori repetitions, the lateral loading pattern is unique to judo and not addressed by standard calf training.

The Fix: Lateral stability training + ankle control exercises specific to the tsugi-ashi pattern. Single-leg lateral hops 2×/week.

The Breakfall Landing

Landing from being thrown — ukemi — creates an impact event through the landing leg before the breakfall slap can absorb it. The sudden loading of the foot-to-ground contact before the slap arm engages places the Achilles under unpredictable acute load.

The Fix: Landing control drills from progressive heights. Single-leg stability to control the first ground contact before ukemi pattern completes.

Randori Volume

Competition preparation typically involves multiple randori rounds with minimal rest. The cumulative Achilles loading from throw entries, footwork, and defensive movements across a 2-hour practice significantly exceeds what isolated strength training can prepare for.

The Fix: Load management between training sessions. Eccentric maintenance 3×/week. Monitor morning Achilles stiffness as a load indicator.
Pre-Training Protocol

Judoka Achilles Activation (10 Minutes)

Complete before every judo session. Covers throw-entry and breakfall demands.

1

Ankle Mobility in Judo Stance (2 min)

Ankle circles and weight shifts in active judo stance. Activates the specific ankle loading pattern used in randori.

2

Tsugi-Ashi Footwork (2 min)

Slow controlled tsugi-ashi steps across the mat at 30% effort. Activates the lateral loading pattern before training intensity.

3

Calf Pumps + Single-Leg Hold (2 min)

20 calf pumps then 30 s single-leg hold per side. Builds the unilateral stability needed for throw entries.

4

Throw Entry Simulation (2 min)

5 slow entry movements per side at 40% effort — focus on the pivot foot mechanics. No throw completion needed.

5

Ukemi Practice (2 min)

3 backward and 3 forward breakfalls at controlled pace. Rehearse landing mechanics before randori intensity.

Training Protocol

Judoka Achilles Strength Block (12 Weeks)

3 sessions per week, 20–25 minutes. Progressive overload every 2 weeks.

Weeks 1–4: Tendon Capacity Base

Eccentric Heel Drops — 3×15 daily, straight-leg on a step. Lower over 4 seconds on one foot.
Isometric Calf Hold — 3×45 s, 3×/week. Rise on toes, hold at top. Builds tendon stiffness.
Seated Calf Raises — 3×20, 3×/week. Knee bent 90°. Targets the soleus specifically.
Single-Leg Balance — 3×30 s per leg. Builds proprioception and ankle stability.

Weeks 5–8: Power Development

Weighted Eccentric Heel Drops — Add 10 lb in a backpack. 3×12 daily. Accelerated tendon adaptation.
Heavy Slow Resistance — 3×12 at 70–80% effort, 3×/week. Maximum collagen-synthesis stimulus.
Pogo Hops — 3×30 s, 2×/week. Rapid ankle-only hops. Builds reactive stiffness.
Lateral Bounds — 3×8 per side, 2×/week. Explosive direction-change loading.

Weeks 9–12: Performance-Specific

Single-Leg Hops for Distance — 3×5 per leg, 2×/week. Power and control combined.
Plyometric Box Step-Offs — 3×6, 2×/week. Step down from 30 cm. Landing control focus.
Sport-Specific Movement Drills — 10 min, 2×/week. Replicate the exact movement patterns of your discipline.
Eccentric Heel Drops (Maintenance) — 3×15, 3×/week. Never stop — this is your performance maintenance.
