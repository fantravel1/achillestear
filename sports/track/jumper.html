Track & Field — Jumper

Protect Your Achilles On the Takeoff Board.

Field jumpers concentrate peak Achilles force into a single explosive moment — the takeoff. Triple jump generates over 10× bodyweight through the take-off foot. Long jump approach speed compounds that force. Here’s how to prepare.

Jumper-Specific Risk

When Achilles Tears Happen in Field Jumping

Each jumping event creates a distinct loading profile. Understanding the specific moment of peak risk in your event drives targeted preparation.

Triple Jump Takeoff

Three consecutive single-leg plantarflexion events — hop, step, jump — each delivering peak forces exceeding 10× bodyweight. The cumulative loading across three contacts makes triple jump the highest Achilles loading event in all of field athletics.

The Fix: Heavy single-leg plyometrics 2×/week + eccentric drops daily. Build capacity progressively across 12 weeks.

Long Jump Approach Speed

Maximum-speed run-up followed by explosive single-leg takeoff at the board. At approach speeds of 10–11 m/s, the combination of velocity and peak force creates an extreme Achilles loading event in a single stride.

The Fix: Progressive approach speed work. Never attempt full-speed approach without adequate tendon conditioning.

High Jump Curved Approach

The curved approach in the Fosbury Flop loads the outside Achilles asymmetrically through the entire approach run. Over a competition season, this creates a consistent bilateral loading asymmetry that predisposes to injury.

The Fix: Bilateral eccentric training — equal reps on both legs. Monitor for stiffness on the curve-approach leg specifically.

Pole Vault Plant

Planting the pole at full approach speed creates a sudden deceleration that transmits enormous force through the plant foot. The Achilles must absorb and redirect this force while the body continues over the bar.

The Fix: Eccentric loading + full-body stability training. Planting mechanics coaching is critical for Achilles protection.
Pre-Training Protocol

Jumper Achilles Activation (12 Minutes)

Complete before every jump session. Progressively loads the Achilles toward takeoff forces.

1

Ankle Mobility + Calf Pumps (3 min)

Circles, alphabet, then 3×30 calf pumps. Full ankle warm-up before any jump-specific loading.

2

Pogo Hops (2 min)

Rapid ankle-only hops 30 s on / 10 s off. 3 rounds. Activates the elastic energy storage critical for all jump takeoffs.

3

Single-Leg Pogo Hops (2 min)

Same as above but single-leg. 20 s per leg, 2 rounds. Directly simulates takeoff mechanics.

4

Approach Build-Ups (3 min)

3×approach at 50%, 70%, 85% speed. Graduated loading before full-effort jump attempts.

5

Takeoff Simulation (2 min)

5 single-leg bounds per side at 70% effort. Simulates takeoff loading without full approach speed.

Training Protocol

Jumper Achilles Strength Block (12 Weeks)

3 sessions per week, 20–25 minutes. Progressive overload every 2 weeks.

Weeks 1–4: Tendon Capacity Base

Eccentric Heel Drops — 3×15 daily, straight-leg on a step. Lower over 4 seconds on one foot. The non-negotiable foundation.
Isometric Calf Hold — 3×45 s, 3×/week. Rise on toes, hold at top. Builds tendon stiffness for high-speed demands.
Seated Calf Raises — 3×20, 3×/week. Knee bent 90°. Targets soleus — often neglected and highly protective.
Single-Leg Balance — 3×30 s per leg. Builds proprioception critical for all track and field movements.

Weeks 5–8: Power Development

Weighted Eccentric Heel Drops — Add 10 lb in a backpack. 3×12 daily. Accelerated tendon adaptation.
Heavy Slow Resistance Calf Raises — 3×12 at 70–80% effort, 3×/week. Maximum collagen-synthesis stimulus.
Pogo Hops — 3×30 s, 2×/week. Rapid ankle-only hops. Trains the stretch-shortening cycle.
Lateral Bounds — 3×8 per side, 2×/week. Explosive side-to-side loading.

Weeks 9–12: Sport-Specific Power

Single-Leg Hops for Distance — 3×5 per leg, 2×/week. Maximise push-off distance, control landing.
Plyometric Box Jumps — 3×6, 2×/week. Step down — don’t jump down. Builds explosion safely.
Sprint Acceleration Drills — 3×20 m at 85% speed, 2×/week. Progressive speed work for tendon readiness.
Eccentric Heel Drops (Maintenance) — 3×15, 3×/week. Never stop. In-season tendon maintenance.
