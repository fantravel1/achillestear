Track & Field — Middle Distance

Protect Your Achilles Over the Middle Distance.

Middle distance runners face the worst of both worlds — sprint-level forces during race kicks, combined with repeated race efforts and demanding speed-endurance sessions. Managing this dual load is the defining challenge.

Middle Distance Risk

When Achilles Tears Happen in Middle Distance

The unique combination of speed and volume creates four distinct loading scenarios that every 800m and 1500m runner must prepare for.

‚ö°

The Race Kick

The final 200 m sprint from race pace to maximum speed creates sprint-level Achilles forces after a race of accumulated fatigue. The tendon is already depleted when it’s asked to produce its highest forces.

The Fix: Eccentric heel drops daily + progressive speed work. Train the kick in practice before racing it.

Multi-Round Competition

Heats, semi-finals, and finals within 24–48 hours mean the Achilles receives repeated high-intensity loading without adequate recovery between efforts. Tendons recover more slowly than muscles.

The Fix: Load management between rounds. Isometric holds only between rounds — no additional plyometric or eccentric loading during competition windows.
üîÑ

Tactical Surging

Repeated accelerations within a race — responding to competitors’ moves — create unpredictable loading spikes at varying points in the race. Each surge demands peak Achilles force under fatigue.

The Fix: Variable-speed training in practice. Progressive race-simulation sessions at varying surge frequencies.
üèÉ

Speed-Endurance Sessions

600 m + 400 m + 300 m race-pace repeats accumulate enormous Achilles loading volume. Done too early in a training block or after insufficient base, they’re the primary source of tendinopathy in middle distance runners.

The Fix: 10% weekly mileage rule. Eccentric heel drops before every speed session.
Pre-Training Protocol

Middle Distance Achilles Activation (10 Minutes)

Complete before every track session and race. Primes the Achilles for race-pace and kick-phase forces.

1

Ankle Mobility Circles (2 min)

10 slow circles each direction per foot. Reduces early-session Achilles stiffness before any loading.

2

Calf Pumps (2 min)

3×30 rapid toe raises, 10 s rest. Increases Achilles temperature and blood flow.

3

A-Skips + B-Skips (2 min)

2×20 m each. Activates the full running chain before race-pace demands.

4

Progressive 200 m Build-Ups (2 min)

2×200 m at 60% then 80% race pace. Graduated loading before any maximal effort.

5

Pogo Hops + Kick Simulation (2 min)

20 pogo hops, then 3×30 m at 90% with an aggressive kick in the final 10 m. Activates the stretch-shortening cycle.

Training Protocol

Middle Distance Strength Block (12 Weeks)

3 sessions per week, 20–25 minutes. Progressive overload every 2 weeks.

Weeks 1–4: Tendon Capacity Base

Eccentric Heel Drops — 3×15 daily, straight-leg on a step. Lower over 4 seconds on one foot. The non-negotiable foundation.
Isometric Calf Hold — 3×45 s, 3×/week. Rise on toes, hold at top. Builds tendon stiffness for high-speed demands.
Seated Calf Raises — 3×20, 3×/week. Knee bent 90°. Targets soleus — often neglected and highly protective.
Single-Leg Balance — 3×30 s per leg. Builds proprioception critical for all track and field movements.

Weeks 5–8: Power Development

Weighted Eccentric Heel Drops — Add 10 lb in a backpack. 3×12 daily. Accelerated tendon adaptation.
Heavy Slow Resistance Calf Raises — 3×12 at 70–80% effort, 3×/week. Maximum collagen-synthesis stimulus.
Pogo Hops — 3×30 s, 2×/week. Rapid ankle-only hops. Trains the stretch-shortening cycle.
Lateral Bounds — 3×8 per side, 2×/week. Explosive side-to-side loading.

Weeks 9–12: Sport-Specific Power

Single-Leg Hops for Distance — 3×5 per leg, 2×/week. Maximise push-off distance, control landing.
Plyometric Box Jumps — 3×6, 2×/week. Step down — don’t jump down. Builds explosion safely.
Sprint Acceleration Drills — 3×20 m at 85% speed, 2×/week. Progressive speed work for tendon readiness.
Eccentric Heel Drops (Maintenance) — 3×15, 3×/week. Never stop. In-season tendon maintenance.
