Protect Your Achilles Over the Long Distance.

Long distance track athletes log the highest training mileage and the greatest cumulative Achilles load of any event. The path from tendinopathy to rupture develops over months — catching it early is everything.

Long Distance Risk

When Achilles Problems Develop in Long Distance

For long distance runners, the risk is cumulative rather than acute. Four patterns drive the tendinopathy-to-rupture pathway.

The Mileage Accumulation

Elite 10 000 m athletes train 120+ km/week. Every kilometre places repeated 2–3× bodyweight loads through the Achilles. Without adequate eccentric maintenance, cumulative stress eventually exceeds the tendon’s repair capacity.

The Fix: 10% weekly mileage increase rule. Mandatory eccentric heel drops 3×/week at all training volumes.
üîÑ

Track Banking Load

Training on the same-direction banked track repeatedly loads the outside Achilles more than the inside. Over months, this creates asymmetric tendon adaptation and a bilateral strength deficit that predisposes to injury.

The Fix: Alternate training direction. Bilateral eccentric loading — equal volume on both legs, every session.

Heat and Humidity Fatigue

Tendon tissue is more susceptible to loading failure at elevated temperatures. Summer racing with high humidity and dehydration compounds the risk. The tendon’s collagen bonds are more vulnerable when systemic temperature rises.

The Fix: Heat acclimatisation protocol over 10–14 days. Maintain hydration. Reduce intensity in early heat exposure.
üìà

Training Through Tendinopathy

The most dangerous pattern: ignoring morning stiffness and reducing pain, assuming it will resolve. Without load modification, reactive tendinopathy progresses to degenerative tendinopathy — and degenerative tendinopathy ruptures.

The Fix: VAS pain >3/10 for 2+ consecutive mornings = immediate load modification. Never push through Achilles pain.
Pre-Training Protocol

Long Distance Achilles Activation (10 Minutes)

Complete before every training session. Prioritises gradual loading over explosive activation.

1

Gentle Ankle Mobility (3 min)

Slow circles, alphabet tracing, ankle pumps. 3 min of gradual range-of-motion work before any loading.

2

Calf Pumps (2 min)

3×30 slow-to-moderate toe raises. Not fast — this is controlled loading to increase tissue temperature.

3

Easy Jog Build-Up (3 min)

5–6 min/km for the first 3 min of every session. No exceptions. Cold Achilles tendons under training pace are high-risk.

4

Single-Leg Balance (1 min)

30 s per leg. Eyes open. Activates proprioceptive stability for high-volume running.

5

A-Skips (1 min)

2×20 m. Coordinates the ankle-calf chain before training-pace loading begins.

Training Protocol

Long Distance Strength Block (12 Weeks)

3 sessions per week, 20–25 minutes. Progressive overload every 2 weeks.

Weeks 1–4: Tendon Capacity Base

Eccentric Heel Drops — 3×15 daily, straight-leg on a step. Lower over 4 seconds on one foot. The non-negotiable foundation.
Isometric Calf Hold — 3×45 s, 3×/week. Rise on toes, hold at top. Builds tendon stiffness for high-speed demands.
Seated Calf Raises — 3×20, 3×/week. Knee bent 90°. Targets soleus — often neglected and highly protective.
Single-Leg Balance — 3×30 s per leg. Builds proprioception critical for all track and field movements.

Weeks 5–8: Power Development

Weighted Eccentric Heel Drops — Add 10 lb in a backpack. 3×12 daily. Accelerated tendon adaptation.
Heavy Slow Resistance Calf Raises — 3×12 at 70–80% effort, 3×/week. Maximum collagen-synthesis stimulus.
Pogo Hops — 3×30 s, 2×/week. Rapid ankle-only hops. Trains the stretch-shortening cycle.
Lateral Bounds — 3×8 per side, 2×/week. Explosive side-to-side loading.

Weeks 9–12: Sport-Specific Power

Single-Leg Hops for Distance — 3×5 per leg, 2×/week. Maximise push-off distance, control landing.
Plyometric Box Jumps — 3×6, 2×/week. Step down — don’t jump down. Builds explosion safely.
Sprint Acceleration Drills — 3×20 m at 85% speed, 2×/week. Progressive speed work for tendon readiness.
Eccentric Heel Drops (Maintenance) — 3×15, 3×/week. Never stop. In-season tendon maintenance.
