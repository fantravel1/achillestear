Achilles Protection for Small Forwards

Small forwards are asked to do everything: cut like a guard, post up like a forward, rebound, run transition, and close out on shooters. That versatility creates multi-directional Achilles loading that no single training pattern can fully address.

Small Forward Risk

Position Demands: Why Small Forwards Are High-Risk

The 3 position asks you to play two roles every possession. Here are the four loading events that create the highest Achilles risk.

Wing Drive to Basket

Baseline and middle drives from the wing require a hard lateral plant to initiate the cut. The planted foot must accept and redirect a full-speed running load in a fraction of a second. Poor deceleration mechanics or a fatigued Achilles can fail here.

The Fix: Eccentric heel drops + lateral deceleration drills. Build the eccentric strength to accept lateral plant forces before redirecting explosively.

Post-Entry Pivot

Catching a post entry pass under pressure, a small forward must pivot quickly against a defender's body contact. The standing leg Achilles is loaded eccentrically as it braces against the pivot, while the body absorbs external pushing force from the defender.

The Fix: Soleus isometric holds (knee bent 90¬∞) 3√ó45 s. The soleus ‚Äî not the gastrocnemius ‚Äî primarily stabilises the ankle in bent-knee pivot positions.

Open-Court Transition

Sprinting the floor as a wing in transition, then cutting at full speed to receive an outlet pass, creates an open-court Achilles loading event at near-maximum sprint velocity. The deceleration plant after a pass catch in transition is one of the highest single-rep forces a small forward faces.

The Fix: Progressive sprint acceleration protocol. Ensure your tendon is adapted to near-maximal sprint demands before preseason transition drills begin.

Offensive Rebound Pursuit

Crashing the offensive glass means explosive vertical jumps from a standstill or mid-movement, often followed by an awkward landing while being bumped by a defender. The landing phase ‚Äî especially off-balance ‚Äî creates uncontrolled force through the Achilles.

The Fix: Box jump landings + single-leg control drills. Train the landing pattern specifically to handle off-balance scenarios that rebounding creates.
Balanced Wing Activation

Small Forward Achilles Warm-Up (8 Minutes)

Balanced between guard cutting movements and forward post/rebound patterns. Do this before every game and practice.

1

Ankle Mobility Circles (2 min)

Single-leg stance, 20 slow ankle circles each direction, both feet. Activates all ankle stabilisers ‚Äî critical for the SF's multi-directional movement demands. Slow and deliberate.

2

Calf Pumps (1 min)

30 rapid toe raises, 3 sets with 10-second rests. Warms the Achilles tendon for the mixed demands ahead ‚Äî cutting, pivoting, sprinting, and jumping.

3

Lateral Deceleration Hops (2 min)

Jog laterally 3 steps, plant hard, hold for 2 seconds. 5 reps each direction. Specifically activates the wing drive deceleration pattern ‚Äî the SF's highest-frequency loading event.

4

Post Pivot Preparation (2 min)

Standing with bent knee, perform 10 slow soleus calf raises (rise and lower over 3 seconds) per leg. Activates the soleus for the bent-knee pivot demands specific to post play.

5

Box Jump Landing Practice (1 min)

Step onto a low box, step off, emphasise a quiet mid-foot landing. 10 reps. Activates the landing mechanics needed for rebound pursuit scenarios.

Pro tip: Do this warm-up BEFORE your team's warm-up. It is your Achilles-specific preparation. Takes 8 minutes. Worth 6‚Äì12 months of season time.

Off-Court Training

Small Forward Strength Protocol (12 Weeks)

Balanced between cutting, jumping, and load management. 3 sessions per week, 20 minutes each.

Weeks 1‚Äì4: Tendon Capacity Base

Eccentric Heel Drops ‚Äî 3√ó15 daily. Straight-leg, slow descent on one foot. The multi-directional SF needs the highest possible baseline tendon capacity.
Soleus Isometric Hold (Bent Knee) ‚Äî 3√ó45 s, 3√ó/week. Knee bent 90¬∞, rise on toes and hold. Targets the soleus specifically for pivot support.
Single-Leg Balance Progressions ‚Äî 3√ó30 s per leg with increasing challenge (eyes closed, unstable surface). Builds proprioception for the SF's varied movement patterns.
Lateral Deceleration Drills ‚Äî 3√ó8 per side, 2√ó/week. Jog to a plant, hold, repeat. Foundation for all wing drive cutting mechanics.

Weeks 5‚Äì8: Multi-Directional Power

Lateral Bounds ‚Äî 3√ó8 per side, 2√ó/week. Explosive side-to-side bounds. Builds the cutting power unique to wing play.
Box Jump + Controlled Landing ‚Äî 3√ó8, 2√ó/week. Jump onto low box, step off with quiet mid-foot landing. Builds rebound landing control.
Eccentric Heel Drops (Weighted) ‚Äî Add 10 lb. 3√ó12 daily. Accelerated tendon adaptation for the SF's high movement volume.
Sprint Acceleration Protocol ‚Äî 3√ó20 m at progressive intensity (60%, 75%, 85%). 2√ó/week. Safely builds the sprint capacity needed for transition play.

Weeks 9‚Äì12: Position-Specific Power

Wing Drive Simulation ‚Äî 3√ó6 per side, 2√ó/week. From athletic stance, lateral jab step, plant, explode to basket at 85%. Direct SF wing drive simulation.
Single-Leg Hops for Distance ‚Äî 3√ó5 per leg, 2√ó/week. Maximum distance, controlled landing. Builds unilateral power for transition cuts.
Rebound Landing Drills ‚Äî 3√ó8, 2√ó/week. Jump from two feet, land on one foot (alternate). Simulates off-balance rebound landing scenarios.
Eccentric Heel Drops (Maintenance) ‚Äî 3√ó15, 3√ó/week. In-season maintenance. Never stop.

Progressive Overload Rule: Increase reps or load 5‚Äì10% every 2 weeks. If pain (not soreness) appears, back off 20% and rebuild gradually. Never skip the eccentric heel drop baseline.
