Position Demands: Why Shooting Guards Are High-Risk

Off-ball movement is deceivingly dangerous. Here are the four moments that put SG Achilles tendons under the greatest stress.

The Curl Cut

Curling off a screen at full speed requires a sharp plant-and-push on the inside foot. At the apex of the curl, the planted foot is under both rotational shear and plantarflexion force simultaneously ‚Äî a particularly dangerous combination for the Achilles.

The Fix: Single-leg landing practice 3√ó10 per side. Teach the ankle to accept load on a planted foot before the cut continues.

Catch-and-Shoot Jump Stop

Receiving a pass in motion, executing a two-foot jump stop, and immediately loading the legs for a shot ‚Äî this sequence compresses an eccentric loading phase into milliseconds. If the Achilles is stiff or fatigued, the jump stop moment becomes a high-risk event.

The Fix: Jump stop landing drills 2√ó/week. 3√ó10 controlled jump stops from various angles, emphasising silent mid-foot contact.

Perimeter Defensive Footwork

Closeout sprints to contest shooters, lateral recovery slides, and fighting through screens all game generate extreme cumulative Achilles volume. The sudden stop-and-restart of each closeout sprint is particularly high-load ‚Äî a cold or fatigued tendon can fail at this moment.

The Fix: 10% max week-on-week volume increase. If defensive intensity spikes ‚Äî preseason, back-to-backs ‚Äî the Achilles needs prior preparation, not surprise loading.

Off-Ball Screen Action

Running through screens all game ‚Äî curl, flare, and back-cut reads ‚Äî creates a high volume of explosive cuts over 32‚Äì40 minutes. No single cut is maximally dangerous, but the accumulated load on the Achilles across the game is significant, especially in the fourth quarter.

The Fix: Eccentric heel drops + proprioceptive training. Build tendon capacity and ankle awareness to handle high-rep cutting volume without fatigue-related risk.
Off-Ball Cutting Activation

Shooting Guard Achilles Warm-Up (8 Minutes)

Focused on off-ball cutting patterns and jump stop mechanics. Do this before every game and practice.

1

Ankle Circles + Foot Intrinsics (2 min)

Standing on one leg, make 20 slow ankle circles each direction. Then, while standing, spread and scrunch your toes 10 times. Both feet. Activates the ankle stabilisers critical for curl-cut mechanics.

2

Calf Pumps (1 min)

30 rapid toe raises standing tall, rest 10 seconds, 3 sets. Increases Achilles tendon blood flow and prepares it for the repeated explosive loads of off-ball movement.

3

Jump Stop Mechanics Practice (2 min)

Walk 3 steps, then execute a controlled two-foot jump stop, emphasising silent mid-foot landing. 10 reps. Progress to a jog, then a run. Train the movement pattern before intensity arrives.

4

Curl Cut Simulation (2 min)

Mark a cone as a screen. Jog around it at 60%, plant hard on the inside foot, and push off in the curl direction. 5 reps each side. Activate the exact muscle pattern used in game-speed curl cuts.

5

Single-Leg Hops (1 min)

10 small hops on one leg, focus on quiet landing. Switch. 2 rounds. Finalises Achilles activation with sport-specific eccentric loading before game-speed movement begins.

Pro tip: Do this warm-up BEFORE your team's warm-up. It is your Achilles-specific preparation. Takes 8 minutes. Worth 6‚Äì12 months of season time.

Off-Court Training

Shooting Guard Strength Protocol (12 Weeks)

Built around off-ball cutting patterns and jump stop mechanics. 3 sessions per week, 20 minutes each.

Weeks 1‚Äì4: Tendon Capacity Base

Eccentric Heel Drops ‚Äî 3√ó15 daily. Straight-leg on a step. Lower on one foot over 4 seconds. Foundation for absorbing the repeated cutting loads of an SG.
Isometric Calf Hold ‚Äî 3√ó45 s, 3√ó/week. Rise on your toes, hold. Builds tendon stiffness needed for jump stop control.
Single-Leg Balance ‚Äî 3√ó30 s per leg, eyes open then eyes closed. Builds proprioception for in-motion catch-and-shoot footwork.
Jump Stop Landing Drill ‚Äî 3√ó10, 2√ó/week. Walk‚Üíjump stop, jog‚Üíjump stop, run‚Üíjump stop. Silent mid-foot landing every rep.

Weeks 5‚Äì8: Explosive Push-Off Power

Single-Leg Hop for Distance ‚Äî 3√ó8 per side, 2√ó/week. Explosive push-off, controlled landing. Builds the push-off power used in all off-ball cuts.
Eccentric Heel Drops (Weighted) ‚Äî Add 10 lb. 3√ó12 daily. Increased load forces faster tendon adaptation.
Lateral Bounds ‚Äî 3√ó8 per side, 2√ó/week. Mimics flare and curl cut mechanics under explosive conditions.
Proprioceptive Board Work ‚Äî 3√ó30 s per leg on a wobble board. Builds the ankle stability needed to handle catch-and-shoot jump stop landings consistently.

Weeks 9‚Äì12: Sport-Specific Cutting Power

Curl Cut Plyometrics ‚Äî 3√ó6 per side, 2√ó/week. Sprint around a cone, plant hard, push off in cut direction at 85% speed. Direct simulation of SG curl cut demand.
Box Jump + Immediate Jump Stop ‚Äî 3√ó6, 2√ó/week. Jump onto a low box, step off, land in a controlled jump stop. Combines explosion and deceleration.
Single-Leg Hops for Distance (Continued) ‚Äî 3√ó5 per leg. Maximum distance, controlled landing. In-season power maintenance.
Eccentric Heel Drops (Maintenance) ‚Äî 3√ó15, 3√ó/week. Never stop. In-season tendon maintenance.

Progressive Overload Rule: Increase reps or load 5‚Äì10% every 2 weeks. If pain (not soreness) appears, back off 20% and rebuild gradually. Never skip the eccentric heel drop baseline.
